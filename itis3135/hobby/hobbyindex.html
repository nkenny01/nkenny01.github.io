About Me


I first began swimming when I was 7 years old. It was the summer going into 2nd grade when my Dad drove me to the pool with my cousin thinking we were going there for fun. However, my dad had other plans for us and signed us up for the summer swim team without us knowing. We didn't have a choice so we decided to stick with it. On my first day, me and my cousin were already passing kids from the age groups above ours even though we had joined late compared to them. We won many ribbons and medals from the swim meets and it was always fun. I joined the swim team every summer afterwards until middle school came along. I got bored of the sport and was more interested in Tae Kwon Do instead so I ended taking up taking a massive break from swimming until I came back in high school.

Once high school came along, my Dad once again forced me to join even though I hated swimming at this point. On my first day, I was the slowest in my group and slower than the age groups below me. I wanted out of the team but I decided to stick with it until it ended. By the end, I was still slow but it did manage to bring back my love for swimming again. Now, I like to swim casually as I don't want a hobby of mine to feel forced but instead be something I enjoy.

Freestyle


Freestyle is one of the main four competitive swimming strokes and the most common stroke you see people use when they are casually swimming. Freestyle is a stroke where your arms come up above water and essentially "pulling" yourself forward against the water and stretching your entire body forward for extended reach while also repeatedly kicking your legs up and down.

Freestyle has always been my favorite stroke as it was my best stroke and overall just fun to perform. It was also fun to watch as freestyle took the least amount of time compared to the others but was always intense as swimmers were always going toe-to-toe with each other. It also translates into casual swimming as people only use freestyle when swimming.

Breaststroke


Breaststroke is one of the main four competitive swimming strokes. Breaststroke is a stroke where you have your hands near your chest underwater and you thrust it forward. After this, you bring your arms back to starting position near your chest by "splitting the water". With kicking, you kick your legs outwards and bring it back by straightening it out first and then slighly bending the knee so that the foot is above your knee.

I was initially really bad at breaststroke when I was kid. I didn't understand the movement and technique of breaststroke, leaving with me times slower than desired. However, as I grew older, breaststroke became easier and I was able to do them easily. It really is a cool stroke to perform as you're imitating a frog (at least that's the analogy my coaches used) and is always fun to watch as well.

Backstroke


Backstroke is one of the main four competitive swimming strokes. Backstroke is a stroke where your back is facing towards the ground and you bring your arms up from the water near your hips and bring it up all the way over until your arm grazes your head while also repeatedly kicking up and down.

I like backstroke as it was always cool to see how people can get such great times while also being elegant in the sense that the water was hardly splashing but rather "gliding" with them. Only thing I don't like about backstroke is the fact that water can easily go up your nose and make you start coughing if your body falls too low under the water.

Butterfly


Butterfly is one of the main four competitive swimming strokes. Butterfly is a stroke where you bring both arms forward simutaneously and kicking "Dolphin" style, where you keep your legs together.

Butterfly was always my least favorite. It's the stroke that requires the most strength and endurance out of you and would always tire me out. Whenever I would go look at my replays that someone had recorded during this event, I would look like a flopping fish trying to get to finish line. It also sucked because it was the first stroke during the individual medley or IM and performing butterfly always puts me in a bad mood.
